Wednesday On-Campus Structure

Every Wednesday session (NCS 242, 10am) follows this structure:

10:00 – 10:20
Mini Lecture — Mentor introduces the week's topic. Concept explanation, key terms, motivation. No hands-on yet — just listen and ask questions.
10:20 – 10:50
Live Coding / Demo — Mentor demonstrates the concept in code. Students follow along on their own machines. The goal is seeing it work before you try it yourself.
10:50 – 11:15
Discussion and Task Assignment — Group discussion of the concept. Mentor assigns the week's specific tasks. Tasks are written down, not just verbal.
11:15 – 4:00
Independent Research — Students work on assigned tasks. Mentor available in NCS 242 or via Slack. 2pm Slack check-in required.

Remote Days (Thursday and Friday)

By noon
Slack progress update — Post in #daily-updates: what you're working on today and any blockers. Keep it to 2–3 sentences.
All day
Independent Work — Assigned tasks, reading, experiments, writing. Async Slack questions answered within 2 hours on weekdays.
Friday 5pm
Weekly deliverable due — Submit via GitHub commit or Google Doc share link posted to Slack.

The Rule of Specific Tasks

At the end of every meeting you will receive a specific task, not a general direction. "Keep working on your project" is not a task. Examples of real tasks:

  • "Run batch_runner.py on 10 rows, find the first wrong prediction, write one sentence about why."
  • "Write 3 adversarial scenarios for the law enforcement HIPAA exception."
  • "Read the Keshav 3-pass paper method and apply it to the Brown et al. GPT-3 paper."
  • "Complete exercises 1–4 in notebooks/prompt_engineering.ipynb."
